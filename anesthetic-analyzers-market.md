<p><p>Anesthetic analyzers refer to devices used to monitor anesthetic gases during surgery or medical procedures. There are two main types of anesthetic analyzers: stationary and portable. Stationary analyzers are larger devices that are installed in a fixed location, such as operating rooms or intensive care units. They provide continuous monitoring of anesthetic gases and related parameters. Portable analyzers, on the other hand, are smaller and lightweight devices that can be easily transported and used in various settings, such as ambulances or dental clinics, to ensure safe administration of anesthesia.</p></p>
