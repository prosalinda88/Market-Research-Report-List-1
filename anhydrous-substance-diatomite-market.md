<p><p>Anhydrous Substance Diatomite is a type of sedimentary rock that is primarily composed of fossilized diatoms, which are single-celled organisms. The market for Anhydrous Substance Diatomite is categorized into three types: Natural, Calcined, and Flux Calcined. </p><p>1. Natural: This type of diatomite is directly mined from deposits and is minimally processed. It is commonly used in filtration systems, agriculture, and as an absorbent material due to its high porosity and absorption properties.</p><p>2. Calcined: Calcined diatomite is subjected to high temperatures, which alters its chemical and physical properties. This type is widely used in the production of cement, paints, and ceramics due to its increased hardness and improved thermal resistance.</p><p>3. Flux Calcined: Flux calcined diatomite is subjected to even higher temperatures, resulting in a more refined and purified product. It finds applications in industries such as metal casting, water treatment, and insulation due to its improved strength, stability, and filtration capabilities.</p></p>
