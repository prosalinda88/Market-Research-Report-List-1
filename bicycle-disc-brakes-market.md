<p><p>The bicycle disc brakes market is divided into two main types: mechanical disc brakes and hydraulic disc brakes. Mechanical disc brakes function by pulling a cable attached to the brake lever, which then activates the brake pads on the rotor to stop the bike. On the other hand, hydraulic disc brakes use fluid pressure to transfer the force from the brake lever to the brake pads. This enables a smoother and more precise braking experience, making hydraulic disc brakes a popular choice among cyclists.</p></p>
