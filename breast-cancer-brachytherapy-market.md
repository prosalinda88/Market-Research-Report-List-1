<p><p>Breast Cancer Brachytherapy is a type of treatment that involves the placement of radioactive sources into the breast tissue near the tumor to kill cancer cells. There are two types of Brachytherapy: High-Dose Rate (HDR) and Low-Dose Rate (LDR). HDR Brachytherapy delivers a high dose of radiation over a short period, typically in multiple sessions. On the other hand, LDR Brachytherapy delivers a lower dose of radiation continuously over a longer period, usually over a few days or weeks. Both types aim to effectively target and treat breast cancer while minimizing damage to healthy tissues.</p></p>
