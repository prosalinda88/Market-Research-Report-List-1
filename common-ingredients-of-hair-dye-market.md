<p><p>The common ingredients of hair dye can be categorized into various market types. Dye precursor refers to the reactive chemicals that produce the actual dye molecule when combined with an oxidant. Coupling agents help in stabilizing and intensifying the color by forming additional chemical bonds. Oxidants play a crucial role in the color development process by facilitating the reaction between dye precursors and coupling agents. The matrix comprises ingredients that provide the desired texture, consistency, and conditioning properties to the hair dye. Other market types may include additives like preservatives, pH adjusters, and fragrances, which enhance the stability, pH balance, and scent of the hair dye.</p></p>
