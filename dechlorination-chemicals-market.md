<p><p>Dechlorination chemicals are used to remove or neutralize chlorine from water. There are three common types of dechlorination chemicals: sulfites, bisulfites, and metabisulfites.</p><p>Sulfites are compounds that contain the sulfite ion (SO3^2-), which reacts with chlorine to produce chloride ions (Cl^-).</p><p>Bisulfites are compounds that contain the bisulfite ion (HSO3^-), which also reacts with chlorine to produce chloride ions.</p><p>Metabisulfites are compounds that contain the metabisulfite ion (S2O5^2-), which reacts with chlorine to form chloride ions.</p><p>All three types of dechlorination chemicals work by chemically reducing or converting chlorine into chloride ions, making water safe for various applications.</p></p>
