<p><p>The electric vehicle (EV) market consists of various types of vehicles, including Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV). These vehicles can utilize different charging methods, such as normal charging, super charging, and inductive charging. Normal charging involves connecting the EV to a standard electrical outlet or a dedicated charging station, which can take several hours to fully charge the vehicle. Super charging, available in some EVs, allows for faster charging times by using high-power charging stations. Inductive charging involves wirelessly transferring energy from a charging pad to the EV's battery, eliminating the need for a physical connection while parked over the pad.</p></p>
