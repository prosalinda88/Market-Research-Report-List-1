<p><p>The fire retardant polypropylene market can be categorized into two types: halogen type and halogen-free type. The halogen type refers to polypropylene materials that contain halogen elements, such as chlorine or bromine compounds. These materials provide effective fire retardancy but may release toxic gases when burned. On the other hand, the halogen-free type uses alternative flame retardant additives that do not contain halogen elements. They offer fire resistance without the emission of toxic gases, making them a more environmentally friendly choice.</p></p>
