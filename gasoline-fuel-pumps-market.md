<p><p>Gasoline fuel pumps are categorized into two main types: mechanical fuel pump and electric fuel pump. </p><p>Mechanical fuel pumps are typically found in older vehicles and operate using the motion of the engine. As the engine rotates, the fuel pump lever is driven by a camshaft, reciprocating to draw fuel from the tank and deliver it to the engine. </p><p>On the other hand, electric fuel pumps are commonly used in modern vehicles. These pumps are powered by an electrical motor, providing a continuous, pressurized flow of fuel from the tank to the engine. Electric fuel pumps are more efficient, reliable, and allow for better control of the fuel flow.</p></p>
