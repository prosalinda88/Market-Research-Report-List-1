<p><p>Heavy hydrogen, also known as deuterium, is an isotope of hydrogen that contains both a proton and a neutron in its nucleus, as opposed to regular hydrogen which only contains a proton. Researchers and companies are actively working on developing nuclear fusion reactors that utilize deuterium, and if successful, it could revolutionize the energy industry.</p><p>Furthermore, heavy hydrogen is also used in the production of heavy water, which is extensively used as a moderator and coolant in nuclear reactors.
