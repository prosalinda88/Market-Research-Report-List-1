<p><p>In the industrial alginate market, two common types are High G and Low G. High G alginate refers to alginates that have a high guluronic acid content, which results in a higher gel strength and viscosity. These types are commonly used in applications where a stronger gel is required, such as in food, pharmaceuticals, and textile industries. On the other hand, Low G alginate has a lower guluronic acid content, leading to lower gel strength and viscosity. These types are suitable for applications where a weaker gel is desired, such as in wound dressings and dental impressions.</p></p>
