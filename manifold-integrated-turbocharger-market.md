This integration also enhances turbo response, reducing turbo lag. Type II involves a separate manifold and turbocharger, offering flexibility in design and installation. Type II turbochargers can be placed farther from the engine, allowing for better cooling and reducing the risk of heat damage. Ultimately, the choice between Type I and Type II depends on specific engine requirements and design preferences.</p></p>
