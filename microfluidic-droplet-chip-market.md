<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The microfluidic droplet chip market can be categorized into two types: passive and active chips. Passive chips are designed to manipulate droplets without the need for external forces. Utilizing principles like capillary action and surface tension, these chips allow for droplet generation, merging, and splitting. On the other hand, active chips require external energy sources, such as electrical fields or acoustic waves, to control droplet movement and manipulation. These chips offer more flexibility and precise control over droplet behavior, enabling advanced applications in fields like biology and chemistry.</p></p>
