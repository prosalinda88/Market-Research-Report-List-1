<p><strong>The Mobile Communication Antennas Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Omni-directional</li><li>Directional</li><li>Semi-directional</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mobile communication antennas can be classified into three types based on their radiation patterns: omni-directional, directional, and semi-directional. </p><p>Omni-directional antennas radiate signals in all directions equally, providing a 360-degree coverage. They are commonly used in urban areas where a wide coverage area is needed, such as cell towers in cities. </p><p>Directional antennas focus their signal in a specific direction, typically with a narrow beamwidth. These antennas are ideal for long-range communication or when the signal needs to be concentrated in one direction, such as point-to-point connections or satellite communication.</p><p>Semi-directional antennas possess characteristics between omni-directional and directional antennas. They offer a moderate coverage area and can focus their signal in specific directions within a limited range. These antennas are commonly used in suburban areas or small cities where coverage needs to be balanced between different directions</p></p>
