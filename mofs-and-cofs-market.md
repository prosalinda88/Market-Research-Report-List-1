<p><p>MOFs and COFs are two classes of porous materials that have gained significant attention in the scientific community due to their potential applications in various industries. On the other hand, COFs, or Covalent Organic Frameworks, are also highly porous materials composed entirely of light elements such as carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen, connected through covalent bonds.</p><p>The MOFs and COFs market has seen substantial growth in recent years, and this trend is expected to continue in the forecast period. The increasing demand for sustainable and eco-friendly solutions in industries such as gas storage, drug delivery, energy storage, and catalysis is driving the market growth. MOFs and COFs offer advantages such as high surface area, tunable pore sizes, and chemical stability, making them ideal for applications in these industries.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that Asia-Pacific is the largest market for MOFs and COFs, owing to the region's growing industrialization, research funding, and presence of major players.
