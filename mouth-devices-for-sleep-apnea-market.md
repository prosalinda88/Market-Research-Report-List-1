<p><p>Mouth devices for sleep apnea, also known as oral appliances, are small devices worn in the mouth during sleep to treat obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) and snoring. Mouth devices have gained popularity as an alternative to continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy, which is often less comfortable for some patients.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements have led to the development of more effective and comfortable oral appliances. Manufacturers have been focusing on the production of custom-made and adjustable devices that provide better fit and adherence, allowing for personalized treatment.
