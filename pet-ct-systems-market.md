<p><p>PET-CT systems refer to the combination of Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Computed Tomography (CT) technologies. These systems are commonly used in medical imaging to provide detailed functional and structural information about the human body. PET scans utilize a small amount of radioactive material to produce images of the body's internal organs and tissue function, while CT scans use a series of X-ray images to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body.</p><p>The PET-CT systems market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving the market growth include the rising prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurological disorders. The integration of PET and CT imaging technologies offers superior imaging quality and accuracy for disease diagnosis and treatment planning, further fueling the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, technological advancements in PET-CT systems, such as the introduction of hybrid PET-CT scanners and software improvements, have enhanced the precision and efficiency of imaging. These advancements have led to increased adoption of PET-CT systems in various applications, including oncology, neurology, cardiology, and orthopedics.</p><p>Rapid growth in the pharmaceutical industry and increasing research and development activities have also contributed to the market growth.
