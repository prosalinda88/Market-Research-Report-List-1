<p><p>Remote Keyless Systems, commonly known as keyless entry systems, are automotive electronic systems that allow users to unlock and lock their vehicles without using physical keys. </p><p>These systems consist of a key fob or remote that communicates with a receiver in the vehicle via wireless technology. <p><ul><li>Passive Keyless Entry Systems</li><li>Active Keyless Entry Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Remote keyless systems, commonly used in vehicles, come in two types: passive keyless entry systems and active keyless entry systems. Passive keyless entry systems automatically unlock the vehicle when the user approaches it, without the need to press any buttons. These systems use radio frequency identification (RFID) technology to detect the presence of the key fob. On the other hand, active keyless entry systems require the user to press a button on the key fob to unlock the vehicle. Both systems provide convenience and enhanced security for vehicle owners.</p></p>
