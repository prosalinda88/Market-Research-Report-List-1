<p><p>In the ICU setting, sedation plays a crucial role in providing comfort, managing pain, reducing anxiety, and facilitating medical procedures for critically ill patients. There are four common types of sedatives used in the market: propofol, dexmedetomidine, remifentanil, and others. Propofol is a short-acting anesthetic that induces deep sedation quickly. Dexmedetomidine is a selective alpha-2 agonist with sedative and analgesic properties, preferred for its ability to maintain patient awakening and reduce delirium. Remifentanil is an opioid analgesic with rapid onset and offset, effective for pain management during ICU procedures. Other sedatives include benzodiazepines, opioids, and neuroleptics.</p></p>
