<p><p>A Standard PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) instrument is a laboratory device used to amplify specific DNA sequences through multiple cycles of heating and cooling. Additionally, advancements in technology have led to the development of innovative PCR instruments with enhanced capabilities, such as real-time PCR and quantitative PCR. These advancements increase the accuracy, speed, and sensitivity of DNA amplification, driving market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the ongoing research and development activities in the field of genomics and genetics further propel the demand for PCR instruments. The ability of PCR instruments to analyze nucleic acid sequences quickly and accurately assists in various areas like pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and agriculture.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing adoption of PCR techniques in forensic sciences and paternity testing also drives the demand for PCR instruments.
