<p><p>The textile wax market is segmented into four types based on color: yellow, green, white, and blue wax. Yellow wax is commonly used for industrial purposes due to its high melting point and resistance to heat. Green wax is preferred for textile printing and coating applications, as it offers great adhesion. White wax is often used for pattern making and as a lubricant in the textile industry. Blue wax is primarily used for wax-resist dyeing, providing intricate designs on fabrics. These wax types cater to various specific needs within the textile sector.</p></p>
